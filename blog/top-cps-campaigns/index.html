Top Performing Global CPS Campaigns of 2026 Every Marketer Should Study

📅 09 June 2026 👤 Vipul Gupta

The CPS (Cost Per Sale) model remains the backbone of affiliate marketing — advertisers pay only when a real sale happens, and affiliates earn a percentage or flat fee on every confirmed transaction. But not all CPS campaigns perform equally. Some convert consistently, pay generously, and align with audiences already in buying mode.

The top CPS campaigns of 2026 span travel, fashion, AI tools, and retail — each thriving because they combine strong brand recognition with products people actively search for and purchase. For marketers evaluating which campaigns deserve their traffic and content investment, these nine global performers represent the strongest opportunities across verticals.

Studying what makes these top CPS campaigns successful reveals patterns — high average order values, trusted brands, seasonal demand surges, and product categories where buyers research extensively before purchasing. Whether you're running campaigns independently or through professional affiliate marketing services, understanding these patterns helps you choose offers that maximize commission potential.

Travel CPS Campaigns Dominating in 2026

Travel CPS campaigns consistently outperform most verticals because average booking values are high and buyer intent is strong.

Klook — Activities and Experiences

Klook monetizes what travelers do at their destination — attraction tickets, tours, transport, and activity packages across 1,000+ destinations globally. While flights and hotels get most affiliate attention, activities represent an underserved opportunity with less competition.

Why it performs: Travelers who've already booked flights and hotels actively search for things to do. This sequential buying behavior means Klook captures high-intent traffic at the final planning stage. Destination guides ("Top 10 Things to Do in Bangkok") naturally embed multiple Klook affiliate links per article.

AirHelp — Flight Compensation

AirHelp is unlike any other CPS campaign because the end user doesn't pay anything. AirHelp helps passengers claim compensation for delayed, cancelled, or overbooked EU flights — earning revenue from successful claims and paying affiliates per qualified submission.

Why it performs: Zero cost to the user means exceptionally high conversion rates. Anyone who's experienced a flight disruption is a potential conversion. Content targeting "flight delayed compensation" or "EU flight rights" captures frustrated travelers ready to act immediately.

Booking.com — Accommodation

Booking.com is the world's largest accommodation platform with millions of properties globally. Its CPS campaign pays commissions on completed hotel stays — and the brand recognition alone drives strong conversion rates.

Why it performs: Booking.com's massive inventory, best-price guarantees, and free cancellation policies reduce purchase friction to near zero. Hotel bookings carry high order values (multi-night stays at $100-$500+/night), generating substantial per-conversion commissions. Affiliates who document their travel campaign performance on their affiliate blog consistently report Booking.com among their highest-earning programs.

Mein Schiff — Cruise Bookings

Mein Schiff (TUI Cruises) targets the German-speaking market with premium cruise experiences. Cruise bookings represent the highest average order values in travel — €2,000-€10,000+ per booking.

Why it performs: Even modest commission percentages on cruise bookings generate massive per-conversion earnings. The DACH market (Germany, Austria, Switzerland) has limited cruise affiliate competition, giving well-positioned content creators a significant advantage. Cruise passengers also have exceptionally high repeat booking rates.

AI and Technology CPS Campaigns Breaking Through

The AI tools market is exploding, and SaaS CPS campaigns offer recurring commission potential that compounds over time.

Sintra AI — AI Digital Employees

Sintra AI offers AI-powered "digital employees" for content, SEO, customer support, and sales automation. The campaign pays 21% commission on sales — one of the highest percentages in the AI tools space.

Why it performs: The AI tools market is projected to reach $539 billion by 2026. Businesses actively searching for automation solutions are high-intent buyers. The current 70% discount creates urgency that drives conversions. Any experienced affiliate agency would confirm that AI tool campaigns are among the fastest-growing CPS verticals this year.

Designs AI — AI Design Platform

Designs AI provides AI-powered design tools for logos, videos, social media graphics, and marketing materials. The CPS campaign pays commissions on subscription purchases from referred users.

Why it performs: Design tools serve a universal audience — marketers, small business owners, content creators, and freelancers all need visual content. The AI angle differentiates from traditional design tools, attracting users specifically searching for AI-powered alternatives to manual design work.

Fashion and Retail CPS Campaigns With Global Reach

Fashion and retail CPS campaigns generate high volume through frequent purchases and seasonal demand spikes.

Target — US Retail Giant

Target's affiliate campaign covers everything from fashion and home decor to electronics and grocery — serving one of America's largest retail audiences. The massive product catalog means affiliates can find relevant products for virtually any content niche.

Why it performs: Target's brand trust drives strong conversion rates on referred traffic. The diverse product range means a single affiliate can promote across categories — home office setup guides linking to Target furniture, back-to-school content linking to Target supplies, and holiday gift guides spanning every department.

H&M — Global Fast Fashion

H&M's CPS campaign targets fashion-conscious buyers across global markets. Affordable pricing, frequent new collections, and strong seasonal promotions make it a high-volume affiliate opportunity.

Why it performs: Fast fashion's low price points drive impulse purchases with minimal buyer hesitation. Seasonal collections create natural content cycles — spring lookbooks, summer essentials, festive outfits — each generating fresh affiliate content angles. H&M's global brand recognition eliminates the "who is this brand?" friction that hurts lesser-known fashion campaigns.

YesStyle — K-Beauty and Asian Fashion

YesStyle specializes in Korean beauty, Asian fashion, and lifestyle products — serving a passionate, repeat-buying global audience. The tiered commission structure pays 3.5% on new users and 1.4% on returning customers.

Why it performs: K-beauty and Asian fashion audiences are deeply engaged and buy repeatedly. Skincare products are consumables that need restocking. Fashion is seasonal. This repeat purchase cycle means a single referred customer generates ongoing commission value. The 30-day cookie captures considered purchases from buyers comparing products.

What Makes These CPS Campaigns Outperform

Across all nine campaigns, common patterns emerge that separate top performers from average CPS offers.

Brand recognition reduces friction. Booking.com, Target, H&M, and Klook are names buyers already trust. Your content introduces the offer; brand familiarity closes the sale.

High order values maximize earnings. Mein Schiff cruises, Booking.com hotel stays, and Sintra AI subscriptions generate substantial per-conversion commissions because the transaction values are high.

Repeat purchase potential compounds returns. YesStyle beauty products, H&M fashion, and Klook activities drive repeat buying — each return visit potentially earning additional commissions.

Seasonal demand creates predictable peaks. Travel campaigns spike during vacation seasons. Fashion campaigns surge during sale events. AI tools see increased adoption during Q1 planning cycles. Timing content around these peaks maximizes conversion rates.

As you build your CPS campaign portfolio across these verticals, working through an established affiliate marketing network provides access to all these campaigns from a single dashboard — consolidated tracking, real-time analytics, and dedicated account management that help you optimize performance across travel, AI, fashion, and retail simultaneously.

Conclusion

The top CPS campaigns of 2026 — Klook, AirHelp, Booking.com, Mein Schiff, Sintra AI, Designs AI, Target, H&M, and YesStyle — represent the strongest earning opportunities across travel, technology, fashion, and retail verticals. Each succeeds because it combines trusted brand recognition, high buyer intent, and product categories where people actively search and purchase.

Study these campaigns not just for what they sell, but for why they convert. Then match the right campaigns to your audience, create intent-driven content, and let the brand trust and product demand do the heavy conversion lifting.
